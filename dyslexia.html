Built for Dyslexia

Structured Literacy That Meets Every Need

The IntraAct method was originally developed to support students with dyslexia and other learning differences. Its design principles align with the best practices recommended by dyslexia researchers worldwide.

  • Explicit, systematic phonics instruction
  • Strong focus on phonemic awareness
  • Repetition and automaticity
  • Clear, structured progression
The Science

Why It Works for Dyslexia

IntraAct addresses the core cognitive challenges that make reading difficult for students with dyslexia.

Reduces Cognitive Overload

Structured, step-by-step instruction breaks reading into manageable components, reducing the cognitive load that overwhelms struggling readers.

Builds Neural Pathways

Systematic repetition and practice strengthen the neural pathways for reading, building the automaticity that dyslexic learners need most.

Strengthens Decoding First

By building solid decoding skills before moving to comprehension, students develop the foundation they need for lasting reading success.

Broader Impact

Effective Across All Learning Profiles

While designed for dyslexia, the IntraAct method produces strong outcomes for a wide range of learners. The same structured approach that supports struggling readers also accelerates typical learners.

  • Students with dyslexia achieve functional literacy
  • Students with ADHD and autism benefit from structured, predictable instruction
  • General education students accelerate faster through systematic phonics
One Pathway for All

One Structured Method. One Consistent System. One Pathway to Literacy for Every Student.

IntraAct eliminates the need for separate programs for different learners. A single, evidence-based method ensures that every student receives the structured instruction they need to become a confident reader.
