How the IntraAct Method Works

A structured literacy approach grounded in cognitive science and the science of reading.

Overview

A Framework for Teaching Reading

The IntraAct method provides teachers with a structured framework for teaching reading that focuses on three core areas: decoding, fluency, and comprehension.

Developed by psychologists Fritz Jansen and Uta Streit, the method is based on cognitive neuroscience research into how the brain learns to read. It translates that science into practical, classroom-ready instruction.

In the Classroom

What Teachers Do

Teachers guide students through 40-minute daily, focused reading exercises that build decoding skills and reading fluency. The method is practical, easy to implement, and fits seamlessly into existing classroom routines.

  • Lead structured, time-efficient reading sessions
  • Provide immediate, supportive feedback
  • Track individual student progress
  • Adapt instruction to student needs
  • Build student confidence through guided success
The Science

Why the Method Works

The IntraAct approach aligns with decades of research in cognitive science and the science of reading.

Cognitive Science

Built on research showing that explicit, systematic instruction builds decoding pathways that lead to automaticity—fast, effortless word recognition essential for fluent reading.

Science of Reading

Aligned with the National Reading Panel's findings on phonemic awareness, phonics, fluency, vocabulary, and comprehension, grounded in decades of interdisciplinary research.

Structured Literacy

Follows structured literacy principles with explicit, sequential, and cumulative instruction designed to ensure mastery and automaticity for all learners.

IntraAct

An evidence-based literacy system grounded in cognitive science, helping educators build confident readers through structured instruction.
