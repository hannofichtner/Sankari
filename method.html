A Simple, Structured Path to Literacy

IntraAct delivers measurable results through consistent daily instruction, structured materials, and real-time progress monitoring.

Daily Instruction Model

40 Minutes a Day, 5 Days a Week

IntraAct is designed for consistent, structured daily instruction that fits naturally into classroom routines, intervention blocks, and summer programs.

  • 40-minute daily sessions build automaticity and fluency
  • 5 days per week ensures consistent practice and retention
  • Designed for classroom, intervention, and summer programs
  • Structured sequences keep instruction focused and efficient

Teachers receive:

  • Clear teaching sequences
Skill Development

Five Core Literacy Skills

IntraAct systematically develops the foundational skills that research identifies as essential for reading success.

Phonemic Awareness

Students learn to hear, identify, and manipulate individual sounds in spoken words, building the foundation for decoding.

Phonics and Decoding

Systematic instruction in letter-sound relationships enables students to decode words accurately and automatically.

Reading Fluency

Repeated practice builds speed, accuracy, and expression, enabling students to read smoothly and with comprehension.

Vocabulary

Students expand their word knowledge through structured exposure and practice, strengthening both reading and oral language.

Comprehension

Students develop the ability to understand, interpret, and engage with what they read through guided practice and discussion.

Mastery-Based Progression

Students Advance Only After Demonstrating Mastery

The IntraAct method ensures no student moves forward with gaps in foundational skills. Each step builds on the last, creating confidence and automaticity.

No Gaps

Students only advance after demonstrating mastery, ensuring no foundational skills are missed.

Confidence

Mastery-based progression builds student confidence and motivation at every stage of learning.

Automaticity

Repeated practice at each level builds fast, automatic reading that supports long-term comprehension.
